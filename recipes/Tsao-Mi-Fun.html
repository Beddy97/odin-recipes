Tsao Mi Fun (Taiwanese Fried Rice Noodles)

Tsao Mi Fun, also known as Taiwanese Fried Rice Noodles, is a popular street food in Taiwan. It is made with thin rice noodles, stir-fried with a variety of ingredients such as pork, shrimp, and vegetables. The noodles are seasoned with soy sauce, oyster sauce, and other seasonings, giving it a savory and slightly sweet flavor. Tsao Mi Fun is a delicious and satisfying dish that is perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz thin rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 oz ground pork
  • 4 oz shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • 2 green onions, sliced

Instructions

  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for 15 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a wok or large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
  4. Add the ground pork and shrimp. Cook until the pork is browned and the shrimp is pink and cooked through.
  5. Add the shredded cabbage and carrots. Cook until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  6. Add the soaked rice noodles, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, and white pepper. Toss well to combine.
  7. Cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring constantly, until the noodles are heated through and well-coated with the sauce.
  8. Remove from heat and stir in the sliced green onions.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy!
